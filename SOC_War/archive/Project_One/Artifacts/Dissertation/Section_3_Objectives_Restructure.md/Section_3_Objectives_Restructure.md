This dissertation explores the limitations of current fitness tracking applications in promoting consistent workout logging and user engagement. It introduces Workout Pro, a cross-platform mobile application designed to enhance gym tracking behavior through focused user interface design and backend support via Firebase.

The core objective of this study is to design, develop, and evaluate a functionally streamlined mobile application that enables users to log and reflect on gym workouts without the distractions or cognitive overhead associated with feature-bloated fitness platforms. By concentrating exclusively on the workout tracking experience, Workout Pro aims to foster habit consistency, reduce friction, and enhance goal adherence among users.

The design philosophy guiding Workout Pro emphasizes functional clarity — delivering only what is essential for the user to complete workout-related tasks efficiently. This research contributes to the evolving discourse in User Experience (UX) and digital health technology, where streamlined functionality is increasingly seen as a mechanism for enhancing usability and retention.

To support this aim, the following specific objectives have been established:

To identify common usability challenges in popular fitness tracking applications, particularly in the context of gym routines.

To design and implement a cross-platform mobile application using Flutter, evaluating its ability to deliver a consistent experience across Android and iOS platforms.

To integrate a backend system using Firebase, a Backend-as-a-Service (BaaS) platform, for secure authentication and real-time data management.

To evaluate the usability, performance, and user satisfaction of the application through controlled testing and structured user feedback.

These objectives give rise to the following research questions:

What usability and engagement challenges are most common among users of current fitness tracking applications, particularly in gym-based contexts?

How can a focused design methodology improve the usability and accessibility of workout tracking features?

What are the key architectural and technical considerations in building a Firebase-integrated, goal-oriented fitness app?

How effectively does the use of a cross-platform framework (Flutter) support consistent functionality, performance, and UX across Android and iOS environments?

To what extent does the application’s focused feature set support regular user engagement and perceived value over time?


These questions guide both the development and evaluation phases of the project,
 ensuring that technical integrity and user-centered effectiveness are systematically addressed.
